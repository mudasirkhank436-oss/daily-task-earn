The Wonders of Science and Biology

3 min read | Science Daily

Science is the systematic study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world through observation and experiment. Biology, a branch of science, focuses on living organisms and their vital processes.

1. The Human Cell: The human body contains approximately 37.2 trillion cells. Each cell is a complex factory that performs thousands of chemical reactions every second.

2. DNA Structure: Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) is the molecule that carries genetic instructions. DNA is composed of two strands that wind around each other to form a double helix.

3. Photosynthesis: Plants convert sunlight into chemical energy through photosynthesis. This process occurs in chloroplasts and produces glucose and oxygen.

4. The Human Brain: The brain contains approximately 86 billion neurons. Each neuron can form thousands of connections, creating a network of trillions of synapses.

5. Evolution: Charles Darwin proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection. Organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring.

6. Microorganisms: Bacteria are single-celled organisms found everywhere. Some bacteria are beneficial (like gut bacteria that aid digestion), while others can cause diseases.

7. Ecosystems: An ecosystem includes all living organisms and their physical environment. Food chains show how energy flows from producers to consumers.

8. Genetics: Gregor Mendel discovered the basic principles of heredity through experiments with pea plants. He established that traits are inherited as discrete units (genes).
